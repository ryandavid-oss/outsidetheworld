How can it get so cold so quickly?  Wait.  How can I complain?

It's been so blasted hot this year, I think we can stand few little sub 50 degree days.  For all of my northern readers (all 2 of you), that sounds a bit odd.  But, let's not be hasty.  50 degrees is still a grimy bit cooler than 110, which is was about two weeks ago.

I really can't say that much happened today; at best, it was very much a cut and dry day.  Went to work, same stuff there, came home, turned on the television, saw that the Lakers were playing.

So, I decided to watch.  Besides, I hadn't yet had the chance to see Malone in a Lakers jersey.  I must admit, I don't like it.  It looks odd, it smells kind of funny, and they don't pass the ball to him enough.  But, they win, and who cares about the rest, eh?

For me, I'll keep watching the Jazz.  Born and bred, I say. 

A big thanks to all of those who are reading my entries daily.  May you have as much fun reading them as I have writing them.

11/04/03

The air is slightly reminiscent of New York in the fall time; the air is crisp, yet breathable, there is a faint scent of cool water that barely crests the breeze
as you wander outside.  It's beautiful, and it hardly lasts here.

When I awoke this morning, the house was cool and dark.  My wife was sound asleep next to me, and the cat was dozing lazily at our feet.  I could faintly hear the distant sound of the welcoming fountain outside of our window, and
I thought to myself: It doesn't get any better than this.

But it does.  My wife made my favorite meal today.  Her "famous to me" sour cream enchiladas, which I must say are the best I have ever tasted.  The gal can cook, I tell you.  She's brilliant.

I had the opportunity to go running tonight, as my doctor had recently cleared me for a slightly increased work out plan.  I decided to go light; I walked outside
of my complex and into the streets.

With the cold air cascading into my lungs, I felt young again.  I mean, I really
felt like I was 16 again, I wanted to run five miles.  As I set off at a slow yet determined pace, I quickly realized what four months had done to me.  My
left leg quickly seized up and caused me to slow to a walk.  It was the
first time my legs had given up before my lungs.

Upon returning home, I sat down feeling rather crestfallen upon my sofa.  I decided to click on the TV to see what I could find, and lo and behold, the Utah Jazz were playing.  This should be a treat, I thought bemusedly.  Most "analysts" has picked the Jazz to finish dead last, not only in the West, but the league entire.

It was a rousing game of lead changes and great basketball.  I was pleased
to see the boys win a game tonight, and not only win, but do it in a fairly impressive fashion.  They definitely aren't your father's Utah Jazz, but they're
fun to watch, and I can imagine watching them a great deal more this season.

That's it for now.  I'm going to go yank the catnip away from the cat.  He's
had more than his fill.
