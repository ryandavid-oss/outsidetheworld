04/13/06

Today was a normal day.  Although my parents did feed me pork chops, which I hadn't had in a few years.  So, it was a normal day + pork chops.  It was a porkmal chopay.  Forgive me.  What?

If I had looked at the clock before I started this entry, I guarantee you I would have thought better.  It's 11:00pm, and I was struggling all day today.  It was an early-rise morning due to physical therapy for my knee.  In reality, I'm just happy that they open up early enough to accommodate me before work.  But still, 6:00am hits me pretty hard.  "Hard" as in the way a car would hit a brick wall dropped in the middle of a freeway.  But my wife said I'm pretty cheerful in the morning, once I wake up.  Too bad it takes me about an hour to wake up. 

My brother in law Matt gets home next Wednesday from Mexico.  He's been there for two years, serving a mission for the LDS church.  I wasn't the most faithful when it came to writing him, but I am quite excited to see the old bean.  He and I are quite alike in more ways than one, and I'm sure he'll want me to give him a clear rundown of the changes in technology since he's been gone. 

I was really going to give you some links to describe the advances in technology, but that's gonna be for another day, m'kay?  I'm just too blasted tired to function on that level.  My late night snack of Cap'n Crunch Berries is quickly wearing off, so I leave you with this:

04/12/06

Earlier this evening, I happened to become thirsty.  It's a normal reaction to a lack of water in the human body, just in case you're not familiar.  So, I took a quick journey to the vending machine to see what interested me.

I honestly didn't feel like soda.  It was late at night, and my body felt the full day's affects by that time, so water sounded good.  I plopped a couple of quarters (read: 2) into the machine and pushed the button. 

$1.00

This has happened to me before, but for some reason, on this night, it was simply baffling.  Utterly bewildering.  Soda cost $.50.  Water cost $1.00.  Why?  Well, you might say that water bottles simply don't sell the same volume as soda does, so the supply and demand simply isn't there.

But my response is, it's just water.

It could be said that the water bottles are bigger, thus taking more space in the machine.  It's necessary to increase the cost to overcome loss of profit from the smaller soda cans.

But my response remains: It's just water.

But you could say that the marketing scheme in America...bup bup bup bup bup!

IT'S JUST WATER.

I feel much better now, thank you.

Turns out he really wants to make the point that he is, in fact, two years old now.  If you cannot make out the fine commentary on this particular video, please let me know, and I'll be more than happy to translate.

I think that'll have to do for now.  I did give you a video, after all.  Only two weeks left of this class.  I'm sure that entries will be up in frequency and quality shortly after that occasion.  Until tomorrow, then.  Take the care.
