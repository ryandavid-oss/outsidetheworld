23 March 2009

04/05/06

After a year long wait, my wife and I finally went to see Billy Joel in concert.  The wait was well worth it, and I knew it would be. Our seats were in section 229 of the America West Arena US Airways Arena, nearly the entire length of way up to the nosebleeds.  But this did not deter us from enjoying every moment of the show.  Well, except those brief moments when the 50,000 watt light bulbs shined right in our faces.  That wasn't so hot.

Billy Joel (it just doesn't sound right to call him "Billy") started the evening off right by playing "Angry Young Man", a track from his Turnstiles album.  After that, it was hit after hit.  He even took the time to turn to our beleaguered section and apologize for the sh!77y seats.  His exact words, mind you. Mr. Joel has always had a salty tongue.  I believe he dropped the S-bomb about 15 times that night.

What's amazing to me is that he hasn't released a new album since 1993, and he doesn't appear willing to break that trend.  As he says in the song The Entertainer, "...I won’t be here in another year if I don’t stay on the charts"  It's a good thing his lack of prophetic insight doesn't affect his popularity.

The highlight of the evening for me was when he sung Vienna.  Ever since I heard that song, I related to it.  Allow me to give you a sample of the lyrics:

Slow down, you crazy child
you're so ambitious for a juvenile
But then if you're so smart, tell me
Why are you still so afraid?

Where's the fire, what's the hurry about?
You'd better cool it off before you burn it out
You've got so much to do and
Only so many hours in a day

But you know that when the truth is told..
That you can get what you want or you get old
You're gonna kick off before you even
Get halfway through
When will you realize, Vienna waits for you?

In this age of time management, multi-tasking, and lunches at your desk, it's wonderful to be able to hear him so eloquently tell you to slow down.  To my knowledge, Vienna symbolizes what waits for you when you're nearly done with your life.  Vienna was where his father finally retired, and he drew great symbolism from that fact, as his father was directly affected by Hitler and the Nazi movement that tore through that country much earlier.

Slow down, you're doing fine
You can't be everything you want to be
Before your time
Although it's so romantic on the borderline tonight
Tonight,...
Too bad but it's the life you lead
you're so ahead of yourself that you forgot what you need
Though you can see when you're wrong, you know
You can't always see when you're right.

You've got your passion, you've got your pride
but don't you know that only fools are satisfied?
Dream on, but don't imagine they'll all come true
When will you realize, Vienna waits for you?

I can't quite explain it fully, but I truly felt extremely young during the entire concert.  Lines like You can't be everything you want to be before your time really mean more to me now.  I reflected on how many people I see around me that are so wound up with life that they forgot what they truly wanted, what they truly needed.   Finally:

Slow down, you crazy child
and take the phone off the hook and disappear for awhile
it's all right, you can afford to lose a day or two
When will you realize...Vienna waits for you?
And you know that when the truth is told
that you can get what you want or you can just get old
You're gonna kick off before you even get half through
Why don't you realize, Vienna waits for you
When will you realize, Vienna waits for you?

Hearkening back to what I wrote a moment ago about time management, it's supremely odd to hear him say it's all right, you can afford to lose a day or two.  So many people have told me time and time again that lost money can be made up for, but time is precious and cannot be regained.  While this is true a majority of the time, it's true that you can afford to lose a day for yourself, especially if the only thing it does is help renew your spirit.

I know Billy Joel is not a spiritual man by no means, but he's got a lot of things right. 

Anyway, sorry for the heavy entry.  I knew it would head this way when I started writing it.  You can't blame me, I'm going on about four hours of sleep.  And yes, that is not enough for me.  Bare minimum of six for normal operation.  But we did eat at Chevy's for dinner tonight.  That place is g-o-o-d good.  Trust me.
